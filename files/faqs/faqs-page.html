If you are registered for the service

1. Log in to EASE https://www.ease.ed.ac.uk/ using your university UUN and password.

2. Access the server from https://rstudio.ppls.ed.ac.uk.


If you forgot your password and want to change it

1. Log in to EASE https://www.ease.ed.ac.uk/ using your university UUN and password.

2. Set your RStudio password here https://rstudio.ppls.ed.ac.uk/setpass.php, the username will be the same as your UUN (make sure you type your UUN correctly).

3. Access the server from https://rstudio.ppls.ed.ac.uk using your university UUN and the password you set in (2).


If you are not registered for the service

1. Log in to EASE https://www.ease.ed.ac.uk/ using your university UUN and password.

2. Set your RStudio password here https://rstudio.ppls.ed.ac.uk/setpass.php, the username will be the same as your UUN (make sure you type your UUN correctly).

2A. If step (2) above didn't work, please complete this form and wait for an email from us. Please note that this can take up to 3 working days.

One you receive an email from us, please follow the following instructions:

  • Set your RStudio password here https://rstudio.ppls.ed.ac.uk/setpass.php, the username will be the same as your UUN (make sure you type your UUN correctly).
  • Access the server from https://rstudio.ppls.ed.ac.uk using your university UUN and the password you just set above.

Go to the LEARN page for your course. On the left-hand side menu, click My Grades and follow the instructions.

The quiz solutions and feedback are NOT immediately available after your submission, only your grade is. For DAPR1, the solutions and feedback are available on the Monday of the following week, after 9am. For DAPR2 and DAPR3 they are available on the Sunday after 5pm.

Go to the LEARN page of your course. On the left-hand side menu, click My Grades. Then, click on the name of the quiz for which you would like to see the question by question feedback. Next, click on your grade and you will be able to see solutions and feedback.

The following is a list of sources of help and support:

  • Labs. Attend the labs to get help and support from tutors on the content of the lab exercises, R and RStudio, as well as lecture contents.

  • Office Hours. Book an Office Hours appointment at this link to get 1:1 help from an instructor. In these help sessions you can ask anything about the course and get more guidance.

  • Discussion Forum. Search on the Discussion Forum, in case another student had the same question and an instructor already answered it.

    You can also ask a new question on the Discussion Forum if no one has already asked the same question. Instructors or other students will be able to provide an answer to your question.

  • Personal Tutor. Your personal tutor should be the first point of contact for generic help and support on matters that are not directly related to this course. They can also advise on generic queries about the degree and the university.

  • TO. The teaching office psyinfo@ed.ac.uk can advise you on teaching-related queries, assessments, extensions, and special circumstances.

  • SSO. The student support office ppls.sso@ed.ac.uk can advise you if you require extra support.

  • Student Counseling. The Student Counselling service lists a wide range of support options.
